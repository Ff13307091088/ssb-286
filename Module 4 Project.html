Project Goals¶

My goal of this project is to use the least factors to make the most accurate prediction to determine if the mushroom is poisonous or edible. The most important goal of my model is to build a "safe" model so that I will not kill anyone. If the mushroom is poisonous, my model will be highly accurate to make the prediction. The second goal is to minimize the wasting. If a mushroom is edible, my model would try not to predict that it is poisonous so that I will not waste the amount of mushrooms. My third goal is to keep my model as simple as possible.

Executive Summary¶

Through using the crosstab method, I find that the odor of the mushroom is a great feature to distinguish if the mushroom is poisonous or not. Also, I find that stalk-color-below-ring is also a good feature to help verify the mushroom. In the crosstab coding section, I found out that there is a strong correlation coefficient between the stalk color below the ring and the stalk color above the ring because the numbers are identical. I also find that we can infer the poisonous of the mushrooms from the gill size because the mushrooms with narrow gill size have a much higher probability of poisonous than the broad gill size mushrooms. For the habitat of mushrooms, I find that the most of the mushrooms growing in the grasses are edible and those growing in the paths are poisonous with a high probability. In the coding section, I found out that the odor of the mushroom is the easiest way to find out whether the mushroom is poisonous or not. From the data, the mushrooms do not have any smell are probably poisonous. Another two dangerous smells are almond and anise. Also, the gill color of buff indicates it is 100% poisonous in this database.

Preliminary Model Analysis¶

My model achieved my 3 goals that I listed in my goal section. The most important one is to construct a "safe" model. If the mushroom is poisonous, my model will be highly accurate in predicting that if it is poisonous to ensure that I would not kill anyone. The second goal is to minimize the wasting. If a mushroom is edible, my model should try not to predict that it is poisonous. My third goal is to keep my model as simple as possible. (crosstab) To achieve my goal, I use the odor and the stalk-color-below-ring as the independent factors to do the predication. Because these two columns have the clear distinctive factors between each category and I use get dummy to classify each column. There are 9 columns for odor and 9 columns for stalk-color-below-ring.(Get_dummies coding steps) Therefore, when you use my model to do the prediction, my model just needs the odor and the spore print color of the mushroom to help made the most accurate prediction. To avoid overfitting our model, I divided the data set into a train and test and I separate my data into 80% for training and 20% for testing.

To make my model close to perfect, I use three models. For the first model, I use 3 hidden layers and one output layer. Each hidden layer has 10 neutrons and activation is relu. For the outer layer, there is only one neutron and the activation is sigmoid. I use the sigmoid function to determine the percentage of the result and it would show the highest result. For the second model, I just reduce my hidden layer into 2 layers and the same output layer. Each Hidden Layer has the same neurons(10) and activation is still the relu. I still use Adam as an optimizer. The output layer uses the same function (sigmoid). For the third model, I want to simplify my model so I just reduce the neurons of my second hidden layers into 2. The other information keeps the same as my second model.
