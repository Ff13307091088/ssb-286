Gini Coefficient¶

Gini is an entropy score of disparity, or difference. According to the decision tree I created and the final gini values, the best categorization occurs on the far right, the second one which has the highest gini score 49.5. That is almost half and half chance of classifying incorrectly. In the contrast, the path that does the best claassification is the one with gini score 0.393 because it is the smallest gini score in the decision tree.

Interpreting the decision tree¶

  • Within each node, the criteria are listed first. The gini value is the measure of entropy. The samples indicate the number of people survived on Titanic. The value indicates [people survived, people not survived]. Recall that overall, 618/(618+427) (59.1%) of all passengers who did not survived.
  • PATH 1: For fare price less than \$52.277 (the upper branch), 558/(558+286) (66.1%) did not survived in this disaster. Compare this to 59.1\% who did not survived. Conclusion: the low fare price has lower chances of getting survive.
    • For passengers' fare less than \$15.646, 379/518 (73.2%) did not survived.
    • For passengers' fare between \$15.646 and \\$52.277, 179/326 (54.9%) did not survived.
  • PATH 2: For fare price greater than \$52.277 (the lower branch), 60/(60+141) (29.85%) did not survived in this disaster. This indicates that higher fare price has a greater chance of surviving.
    • For pessengers who is less than 60.5 years old AND paied a higher price for fare, 53/190 (27.89%) did not survived. Conclusion: age under 60.5 has the greater chance to survive.
    • For pessengers who is mmore than 60.5 years old AND paied a higher price for fare, 7/11 (63.6%) did not survived.

Prediction¶

To make prediction, I made a confusion matrix shows the model's prediction. The diagonal from upper left(565) to lower right(137) are the accurate predictions. We predicted 201 correctly for those people who died and they are "true negatives". The second row which represents the number of people survived and we predict 137 correctly. They are "true positives". The number in the upper right corner represents people who died but was falsely predicted as survived which is 53. The number in the lower left corner represents people who survived but was falsely predicted as died which is 290.

In order to fully evaluate our prediction, I create three passengers who would have been on the titanic. The first passenger is 22 years old and pays \$65 for the fare. The second passenger is 30 years old and pays \$10 for the fare. The third one is 65 years old and pays for \$55 for the fare.

The predict method returns the class for the prediction. The predict probability method class probabilities of the input samples X. The predicted class probability is the fraction of samples of the same class in a leaf. In this case, since 0 indicates not survive and 1 indicates survive. For the first passenger, this indicates a positive result since the result is 1. My model predict that this passenger has 72.1\% chance of survive. For the second passenger, this indicates a negative result since the result is 0 and my model predict that the passenger has 26.8\% chance of survive. For the third person, it is also a negative result and my model predict that chance of survive is 36.4\%.
